Blaming

Blaming is the feeling that things you do wrong or that go wrong for you are always the fault of others. If you forget something, it must be that others didn’t remind you. If you make a mistake, it was cause someone else distracted or confused you. Blaming is the unwillingness to take responsibility for your own actions.

Challenge your concepts of blame. Remember that you are the only one responsible for your actions and that it is unhealthy and false to be angry at others for what you did. Understand that you ascribing blame to others is simply keeping you from learning from what mistake you made. Similarly, understand that some bad things that happen to you are no one’s fault.

When you begin to blame other people, stop and say to yourself that you are not going to ascribe your actions or happenstances to other people. Remind yourself that you are going to move forward away from your situation and blaming others will not make things easier.
