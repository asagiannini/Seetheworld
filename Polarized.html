Polarization

Polarized thinking is the belief that the world is divided simply into black and white. Events, people, and thoughts are categorized as good or bad. There is no subtlety or nuance as every action you do can be viewed only as success or failure.

Challenge your polarizing thought. Remember that the world doesnâ€™t cleanly divide in two. Something you said, did, or saw may seem entirely bad to you, but in reality it is likely a minor or even a good thing.

When you feel that things are only one of two ways, learn to automatically challenge this thought by admitting that it likely is some good and some bad. Learn to stop yourself when you begin to try dividing things cleanly in two.
