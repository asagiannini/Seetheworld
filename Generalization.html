Overgeneralization

Overgeneralization is mistakenly assuming that any one or two incidents in your life are indicative of a greater pattern. If you have a bad experience doing something, you might automatically assume that every time you do that thing, it will be the same. One-time mistakes may lead you to think youâ€™re never going to be good at a task or are bad at every other similar task.

Challenge your generalizations. Remember and tell yourself that any single instance from your life does not necessarily imply much about life as a whole. Take each instance alone and learn to shake off the feeling that your feelings about the wider world are meaningful.

When you are tempted to generalize, make yourself stop and remember that you are dealing with one discrete case. Ignore the feeling that it is significant and tell yourself that you will keep moving forwards.
