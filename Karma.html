Karma

The karma fallacy is the belief that whenever we do something difficult, we will eventually be rewarded for it. It’s the belief that when you do something that you have to work for, the world or people around you will eventually recognize and reward you for it. You will be angry if this doesn’t happen because you so clearly deserve it.

Challenge your belief that every action will be rewarded. Many difficult actions in life come without reward and it is not realistic to expect an even payoff for everything you do. There is no point in being angry or bitter about it, as you can’t control how the world is.

When you feel entitled to a reward or angry that you haven’t received one, remember that you won’t necessarily receive anything. You should be happy at your own strength of character when doing difficult things, and tell yourself to not wait for a reward but keeping working hard.
