Mislabeling

Mislabeling is a form of faulty generalization where you make an exaggerated and dramatic assessment based on little to no information. If you see your coworker taking a longer than normal lunch break, you might begin to think of him as lazy or a bad employee. If someone corrects you, you might begin thinking their a know-it-all or out to get you. Mislabeling is believing something extreme without having a good reason.

Challenge your desire to label. If you are thinking about someone or something in an exaggerated or dramatic way, remember that you are likely going too far and may not be thinking things out. Remind yourself that labels should rarely be applied quickly or angrily.

When you begin to think of things in loaded terms, stop yourself and challenge the idea that these labels fit. Consider what might be a more appropriate way of thinking and aim to do so, without labels.
