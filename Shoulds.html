Shoulds

This distortion refers to the belief that you and everyone else should act according to your personal rules. If you believe something should be done in a specific way, others not doing it as such will feel like a violation. If you do something that breaks one of your own rules, you feel as if you’ve gone against a law or principle. In other words, your own rules become an inflexible set of guidelines you don’t want yourself or others to vary from.

Challenge your belief in what people should do. Understand that in many situation how people go about things is different from you and none of your business. Remember that when you go against your own rules, you are not necessarily doing anything irrevocably wrong.

When you start to object to the behavior of others or your own new action, remind yourself that your rules are personal and do not always have to apply. Tell yourself that how other people act is up to them, and that your rules are only good in so much as they are helpful.
