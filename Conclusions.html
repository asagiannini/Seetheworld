Jumping to Conclusions

Jumping to Conclusions is exactly what it sounds like. Presented with no evidence, you have a tendency to rush forward based on your own faulty guesses. Instead of generalizing based off of an incident, you tend to move forwards without any real reason. A feeling of fear or anxiety may make you assume that people don’t like you or that you will fail.

Challenge your conclusions. Learn to tell yourself when you don’t have any evidence for your negative beliefs. When you feel like you know what is going on or what is going to happen, remind yourself that this is a false feeling.

When you feel yourself coming to a conclusion, stop and remind yourself of exactly how little evidence you have. Learn to ignore the anxiety that is unfounded and keep going despite what you might fear.
