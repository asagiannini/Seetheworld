Fallacy of Change

The Fallacy of Change is the belief that others should change to suit what you want or need. It puts the responsibility for your happiness and wellbeing on other people. When you are unhappy or unable to accomplish something, you will blame the inflexibility of other people and their unwillingness to change their behavior to help you out.

Challenge your expectation of other people. It is not accurate to expect other people to change around you depending on what you want or need. You need to take responsibility for making yourself happy outside of (or despite) whether people change at all.

When you feel that other people should be changing to help you out, stop and tell yourself that only you are responsible to change if it will help you out. Remind yourself that others are not responsible for what you want and that you can get on well among them without any change on their part.
