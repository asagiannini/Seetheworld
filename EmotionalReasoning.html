Emotional Reasoning

Emotional reasoning is the belief that how you feel about something is indicative of what is true. If you feel sad, it must be because the world is against you. If you feel insecure, it must be because you recognize accurately all of your faults. Emotional reasoning keeps you from being able to move past your emotions in order to deal with the real world.

Challenge your emotional reasoning. Remember that your feelings only tell you that you feel a certain way at the moment. Remember that the real world is not affected by how you feel and that feelings don’t say anything about you as a person.

When you feel strongly emotional, remind yourself that what thoughts you might currently have about the world or your circumstances are not accurate depictions of what is going on. Remind yourself that while you can’t always control how you feel, you can ignore the faulty beliefs that come with emotions.
